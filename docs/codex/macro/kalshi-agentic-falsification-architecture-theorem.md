**Authoritative architecture doctrine for the Kalshi signal factory.**

This document reproduces the formal theorem in full. It supersedes the earlier

## Definitions

Let the following quantities be defined for the Kalshi event-contract venue:

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| $\mathcal{M}$ | The set of active Kalshi event-contract markets |
| $m \in \mathcal{M}$ | A single market (a binary YES / NO contract) |
| $p_{\text{market}}(m)$ | The crowd-implied probability of YES, derived from the order-book midpoint (or last executable price) |
| $\hat p(m)$ | A model-estimated probability that the contract settles YES, produced by a signal family |
| $\{\alpha_k\}_{k=1}^{K}$ | A set of $K$ candidate alpha hypotheses generated by an agent (human or machine) |
| $\text{cap}(m)$ | The maximum position size (in contracts) executable in market $m$ before price impact erodes the edge, given the visible order-book depth |
| $\Sigma$ | The $K \times K$ correlation matrix of edge realizations across markets that share a common driver (same game outcome, same macro release, same asset) |
| $w \in \mathbb{R}^K$ | A position vector over $K$ candidate signals |
| $\text{FDR}$ | False discovery rate — the expected proportion of rejected null hypotheses that are false positives |
| $\text{BH}(q)$ | Benjamini-Hochberg procedure at level $q$: sort $K$ p-values ascending, reject $H_i$ iff $p_{(i)} \le \frac{i}{K} q$ |
| $\Pr[\text{cap}(m) > 0]$ | The ex-ante probability that a randomly chosen Kalshi event contract has any executable depth at all at the time of evaluation |

## Axioms

### Axiom 1 (No Discretion)

> The map $(\hat p, p_{\text{market}}, \Sigma, \text{cap}) \to w$ contains no
> human-valued parameter. Every parameter in the pipeline is a deterministic
> function of its inputs, fit once on a frozen historical window or derived from
> a mechanical rule. Humans may configure policy (thresholds, data contracts,
> safety gates) but must not steer individual entries, exits, sizing, or
> hypothesis selection at inference time.

**Rationale.** Discretion introduces latent degrees of freedom that cannot be
backtested or falsified. If a human can override the model then the model's OOS
evidence does not apply to the override decision. The only way to accumulate
replicable edge is to fix the decision function and evaluate it as a black box.

**Operationalized as.** Prediction rules are mechanical: `crypto_prediction_rule`
(above/below -> YES/NO), `sports_prediction_rule` (sigmoid strength -> YES/NO at
threshold $\tau$), `weather_prediction_rule` (bracket probability vs ask). Model
coefficients are fit once on a holdout history and frozen. Gate thresholds
(`fdr_alpha=0.10`, `min_independent_labels=30`, `max_cluster_share=0.35`) are
configured at the system level, not per-decision.

---

### Axiom 2 (Breadth Dominance)

> For fixed aggregate edge, expected log-growth is increasing in $K$ and
> decreasing in the average magnitude of any individual edge. $K$ weak,
> uncorrelated signals dominate one strong signal at equal aggregate edge.

**Rationale.** This is standard portfolio-theoretic diversification, but applied
at the signal-discovery level rather than the position-sizing level. The
expected growth rate of a log-utility investor satisfies

$$\mathbb{E}[\log(1 + w^\top r)] \approx w^\top \mu - \frac{1}{2} w^\top \Sigma w$$

for small $w$. Spreading edge across $K$ uncorrelated signals (off-diagonal
$\Sigma$ near zero) reduces the variance penalty quadratically in $K$ while
preserving the linear return term. Therefore the mission adds **families**
(sports, weather, macro_econ) rather than concentrating in one family.

**Operational consequence.** The signal factory is evaluated on breadth (number
of independent signal families passing FDR, number of surviving hypotheses, number
of independent clusters with positive depth) rather than on the Sharpe of any
single hypothesis. Signal generation capacity — the rate at which $K$ grows — is
a first-order architectural concern, not a nice-to-have.

---

### Axiom 3 (Capacity Boundedness)

> $\text{cap}(m)$ is small and exogenous. The venue imposes structural limits
> on executable position size that bind regardless of the operator's preferences.
> Specifically:

> $$\Pr[\text{cap}(m) > 0] \approx 0.18$$

> and, conditional on positivity,

> $$\text{cap}(m) \in [\$5{,}000,\; \$15{,}000]$$

> in notional value for the overwhelming majority of markets.

**Rationale.** Unlike a discretionary hedge fund (which chooses to cap AUM at
\$10B because it believes liquidity degrades beyond that point), this bound is
**venue-imposed**. Kalshi event contracts are retail-sized; most have thin
order books. Zero executable depth is the DEFAULT state of a Kalshi market,
not an anomaly. Even where depth exists, it is small. This structural constraint
is coded in the validation contract as **VAL-SGATE-058** and is binding on all
sizing decisions.

**Operational consequence.** Because $\text{cap}(m)$ is small regardless of
operator preference, the only way to deploy meaningful capital is through
**market count** — K large — not through concentrated position size. This
reinforces Axiom 2: breadth is not optional.

## Lemmas

### Lemma 1 (Kelly, not Markowitz)

> Correlation-adjusted fractional Kelly is the correct local portfolio
> objective. Mean-variance optimization (Markowitz) is a second-order Taylor
> approximation to the Kelly criterion and is not the primitive.

**Proof sketch.** Let a Kalshi position be a vector $w$ of contract quantities,
each with return $r_i \in \{-1, +1\}$ (binary, unlevered, discrete). The
log-wealth after one period is

$$W' = W (1 + w^\top r), \quad \mathbb{E}[\log W'] = \log W + \mathbb{E}[\log(1 + w^\top r)].$$

For small $w$ (justified by Axiom 3), expand about $w=0$:

$$\mathbb{E}[\log(1 + w^\top r)] = w^\top \mu - \frac{1}{2} w^\top \Sigma w + O(\|w\|^3)$$

where $\mu = \mathbb{E}[r]$ and $\Sigma = \text{Var}(r)$. This is exactly
mean-variance with $\lambda = \frac{1}{2}$ (the Kelly-implied risk-aversion
coefficient). **Markowitz is the local quadratic surrogate for Kelly**, not an
independent criterion. Correlation-adjusted fractional Kelly (the full
$\arg\max$ of $\mathbb{E}[\log(1 + w^\top r)]$ under capacity constraints) is
the correct objective.

**Remark (Kalshi recovers Kelly's native case).** Kelly's 1956 construction
was a discrete, unlevered, binary wager with known probabilities and a fixed
payout. Kalshi contracts are exactly that: binary, unlevered, discrete, with a
known payout at settlement ($0$ or $1$ per contract). Thus Kalshi **recovers**
Kelly's native case without the approximations needed for continuous finance.
Fractional Kelly (a cautious fraction of the full Kelly stake) is the natural
extension to handle estimation error in $\hat p$.

**Mission scope boundary.** This Lemma specifies the eventual sizing objective.
The Kelly layer — the map $\hat p \to w^*$ — is **blocked in this mission**.
Every artifact carries `usable=false`; the transition from calibrated probability
to position size is explicitly deferred. The Kelly computation is the eventual
post-mission goal.

### Lemma 2 (The Significance Bar Rises in K)

> Under the global null (no true edges), the expected number of false positives
> at per-hypothesis level $\alpha$ is $K\alpha$, which is unbounded as
> $K \to \infty$. Holding the false discovery rate constant via the
> Benjamini-Hochberg procedure forces the effective per-hypothesis threshold to
> shrink as $K$ grows.

**Proof sketch.** Under the null, $K$ independent p-values are $U[0,1]$.
Expected significant findings = $K\alpha$ at any fixed $\alpha$, so the absolute
number of false positives grows linearly with $K$. The BH procedure at level $q$
rejects when $p_{(i)} \le \frac{i}{K}q$. The most significant p-value must
satisfy $p_{(1)} \le \frac{q}{K}$, which shrinks ~ $1/K$. For large $K$, the
survival bar thus rises:

$$\lim_{K \to \infty} \frac{q}{K} = 0.$$

**Remark (agentic generation makes the gate the binding constraint).**
Historically $K$ was bounded by human analyst headcount: an analyst team could
generate maybe $10^2$ hypotheses per year. Agentic signal generation removes
that bound — $K$ is now bounded only by compute. Lemma 2 says that as $K$ grows,
the FDR gate does not become optional; it becomes **the binding constraint** on
the entire pipeline. Without the gate, noise dominates. With the gate, only
hypotheses with very strong evidence survive.

**Operationalized as.** The `benjamini_hochberg` helper in `predmarket.shared_helpers`
implements exactly this procedure. The mission uses `fdr_alpha=0.10` across all
signal families. Because adding families increases $K$, the effective significance
bar rises as the signal factory adds breadth — which is correct behavior, not a
bug. It ensures that breadth comes from genuinely replicated signal, not from
expanded false-positive opportunity.

## Theorem (The Minimal Construction)

> The minimal architecture that satisfies Axioms 1-3 and respects Lemmas 1-2 is:

```text
Order Book + External Data ---> Features ---> Agent ---> {alpha_k}_{k=1..K}
                                                              |
                                                              v
                                                     Falsification Gate
                                                              |
                                                              v
                                                           p_hat
                                                              |
                                                              v
                                                           Kelly
                                                              |
                                                              v
                                                           w^*
                                                              |
                                                              v
                                                          Orders
                                                              |
                                                              v
                                                     P&L_{t+h}
                                                              |
                                                              v
                                                        Decay Update
```

Formally,

$$w^* = \arg\max_{w} \Big[w^\top (\hat p - p_{\text{market}}) - \lambda\, w^\top \Sigma w\Big]$$

subject to:

$$0 \le w_i \le \text{cap}_i \quad \forall i$$

where:
- $\hat p$ is the vector of model-estimated probabilities surviving the falsification gate
- $p_{\text{market}}$ is the vector of crowd-implied probabilities
- $\lambda$ is the Kelly-implied risk-aversion coefficient (exactly $\frac{1}{2}$ for the quadratic approximation; see Lemma 1)
- $\Sigma$ is the edge correlation matrix
- $\text{cap}_i$ is the capacity bound for market $i$ (see Axiom 3)

**Feasibility.** The constraints $0 \le w_i \le \text{cap}_i$ always admit the
feasible solution $w = 0$ (do nothing). The objective is concave in $w$ (the
quadratic penalty is convex), so the maximizer $w^*$ exists and is unique when
the feasible set is non-empty. The composition
$\{\alpha_k\} \to \text{FDR gate} \to \hat p$ ensures that only hypotheses
surviving a significance bar ~ $q/K$ contribute to $\hat p$.

**Minimality.** The gate (Lemma 2) and the capacity constraint (Axiom 3) are
each load-bearing. Neither is a refinement:

- **Omit the gate.** Then $\hat p$ includes all $\{\alpha_k\}$, including those
  that are noise under the null. As Lemma 2 shows, expected false positives =
  $K\alpha$, unbounded. For large $K$, noise dominates signal and $w^*$ is a
  random vector with negative expected log-growth.

- **Omit the capacity cap.** Then $w^*$ may allocate $\text{cap}_i = \infty$ to
  a market where the true executable depth is \$5K. The solution is
  **unexecutable** — the orders would not fill at the prices used in the
  optimization, and the realized P&L would diverge arbitrarily from the planned
  P&L.

Thus both constraints are necessary for the architecture to produce a
non-degenerate, executable portfolio. They are the minimal set; no third
constraint is required at the architectural level.

## Corollary (Sequencing)

> The false-discovery estimate stabilizes fastest on fast-resolving data.
> Therefore the decomposition into families should sequence by resolution
> frequency — instantiate the fastest-resolving family first, then admit
> slower categories only once the FDR estimate has stabilized on the
> faster-resolving data.

**Rationale.** Lemma 2 shows the gate depends on $K$ and on the observed
p-value distribution. That distribution converges to its true shape only after
many independent resolution events. Sports props resolve in hours and provide
~30-45 independent binary outcomes per day in-season. Weather contracts resolve
daily. Macro-economic and corporate-event contracts resolve quarterly or
episodically.

**Binding mission order.** This theorem directly determines the mission
sequencing:

1. **Sports (baseball) first** — fastest resolution, most independent events
   per unit time, smallest correlation clusters. Build the sports lane before
   any slower-resolving family.
2. **Weather second** — daily resolution, moderate independence (stations
   within a region are correlated, but cross-region events are independent).
3. **Macro-econ third** (deferred) — quarterly resolution, few independent
   events per year. The FDR estimate will take years to stabilize.
4. **Politics deferred** — episodic resolution, severe correlation across
   related contracts, very low $K$ per unit time.

**Gate discipline.** "Admit slower categories" means the system must demonstrate
a stable FDR estimate — defined as three consecutive multi-family status reports
where the gate rejection count does not change by more than 10% — on
faster-resolving data before the first slower-resolving contract is admitted to
the pipeline.

## Closing Remark

> The object of value in this architecture is not any single hypothesis, not any
> particular calibrated probability, and not any individual position. **The
> object of value is the operator itself** — the self-improving research factory
> that generates $\{\alpha_k\}$, falsifies candidates, retires decayed signals,
> and produces a stream of executable, capacity-aware, correlation-diversified
> positions.

The architecture is a **machine for producing $\hat p$ vectors**. Each cycle —
features $\to$ falsification $\to$ Kelly $\to$ P&L $\to$ decay update —
improves the operator's estimate of where the crowd is wrong. The value is in
the compounding: each cycle's evidence updates the next cycle's priors. No
individual position is valuable; the process that generates the positions is.

This is why the axioms forbid discretion (Axiom 1), demand breadth (Axiom 2),
and respect the venue's structural constraints (Axiom 3): the operator degrades
if humans intervene, if it concentrates, or if its positions cannot be filled.
The theorem's minimal construction is the smallest machine that satisfies these
three inviolable constraints.

## Mission Scope Boundary

The theorem's full construction includes the Kelly sizing map $\hat p \to w^*$,
order generation, execution, and P&L feedback. **This mission stops at the
falsification gate** — the final artifacts are calibrated probabilities
($\hat p$) with capacity/correlation/decay gate evidence, but no $w^*$ is
computed. The Kelly layer is the eventual post-mission goal.

**Blocked in this mission:**
- Kelly / fractional Kelly position sizing
- Order generation of any kind
- Execution of any kind
- Account or order-path code
- Any `usable=true` row

Every artifact in this mission carries `usable=false`, `research_only=true`,
`execution_enabled=false`. This boundary is a hard contract and is asserted in
the validation contract and in tests.
